## What's in the Proposal

**The ask:** $1,000 annual budget for AI tool subscriptions and emerging tool evaluation.

**Tool stack:**

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| Perplexity Pro | Real-time cited research, fact-checking, competitor monitoring | $200/yr |
| Gemini Pro | Google Workspace integration for operational tasks | $240/yr |
| Picryl Premium | Full-resolution public domain imagery from Library of Congress, Smithsonian & National Archives | $120/yr |
| Emerging tools testing | Travel & tourism AI evaluation and vendor trials | ~$440 |
| **Total** | | **$1,000** |

**Note on Claude:** Claude is not included because Visit Loudoun already has access through the organization's AI coursework and will likely move to a Claude Enterprise subscription. This budget covers the complementary tools a single enterprise platform cannot replace.

**Key arguments made in the proposal:**
- Industry benchmark: the average company spends $6,000/yr on AI tools — this ask is one-sixth of that
- Marketers using AI recover 6+ hours per week (HubSpot); at any reasonable labor rate the ROI is 6–8x
- 49% of DMOs say content creation is where AI delivers the most value (Sojern 2024)
- Different tools surface different perspectives — multi-tool research is a quality control practice, not redundancy
- Staying current on a fast-moving industry requires active tool use, not second-hand reading
- This budget funds the evaluation period that makes a future org-wide platform decision informed rather than a guess
